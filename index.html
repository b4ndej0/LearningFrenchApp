French Grammar Practice

Que vs Dont

"Que" is used as a direct object, while "dont" is used for objects of prepositions, especially "de".

Example: Le livre que j'ai lu. (The book that I read.)

Example: Le livre dont j'ai besoin. (The book that I need.)

Qui vs Que

"Qui" is used for subjects, while "que" is used for objects.

Example: L'homme qui parle. (The man who is speaking.)

Example: Le livre que j'ai lu. (The book that I read.)
